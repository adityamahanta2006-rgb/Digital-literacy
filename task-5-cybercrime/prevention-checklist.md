# 'Stay Safe Online' Checklist: Student Edition

As a Digital Ambassador, I recommend these 8 actionable tips to protect yourself from cyber threats in India:

1. **The Golden Rule of UPI:** Remember that you **never** need to enter your UPI PIN to *receive* money. If an app asks for a PIN, money is leaving your account.
2. **QR Code Caution:** Never scan a QR Code sent by a stranger. QR codes are for *making* payments, not receiving them.
3. **Verify Official Apps:** Only download banking or payment apps from the official Play Store or App Store. Avoid "Modded" versions.
4. **Double-Check UPI IDs:** Before hitting 'Send' on any high-value transaction, verify the recipient's name that appears in the app.
5. **Two-Factor Authentication (2FA):** Enable MFA on your email, LinkedIn, and Instagram to prevent identity theft.
6. **Public Wi-Fi Hygiene:** Avoid logging into bank accounts or making UPI payments while connected to free college or cafe Wi-Fi. Use a VPN or mobile data.
7. **Social Media Privacy:** Do not share your birthday, phone number, or home address in public "About" sections to prevent social engineering.
8. **Report Immediately:** If defrauded, immediately call the **National Cyber Crime Helpline at 1930** or report the incident at **[cybercrime.gov.in](https://cybercrime.gov.in)**.
