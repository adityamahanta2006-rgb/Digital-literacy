# Personal Reflection: Cybercrime Awareness & Prevention

### **Key Discovery**
While researching **UPI/Online Payment Fraud** in India, I was most surprised by how effectively attackers weaponize psychological triggers like **'urgency'** and **'greed'** to bypass a user's logical thinking. It was a sobering realization that even tech-savvy B.Tech students can fall victim if they are unaware of a simple technical fact: **a QR code is designed only to send money, never to receive it.**

### **Behavioral Changes**
As a direct result of this research, I am implementing the following changes to my personal digital habits:

1.  **Authentication Upgrade:** I realized that relying solely on passwords is a major vulnerability. I have now transitioned to **app-based authenticators** (like Google Authenticator or Microsoft Authenticator) for my primary academic and financial accounts.
2.  **UPI Vigilance:** I have committed to a strict rule of **never entering my UPI PIN** unless I am the one intentionally initiating a payment. I will no longer engage with "request" notifications or QR codes from unverified sources.

### **The Ambassador's Role**
Understanding these psychological triggers and technical loopholes is essential for my role as a **Student Digital Ambassador**. My goal is to help my peers stay vigilant by simplifying these complex threats into actionable safety rules, ensuring our batch remains secure in an increasingly digital economy.
