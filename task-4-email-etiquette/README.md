# Reflection: The Impact of Poor Digital Communication

### **The Scenario**
A common scenario where poor digital communication causes significant issues is the **"Incomplete Subject Line"** or **"Informal Tone"** when emailing faculty. For instance, a student might send an email titled *"Help"* without providing their name or registration number, and using text-speak like *"plz check my file."*

### **The Consequences**
In this situation, the professor may ignore the email because it looks unprofessional, lacks context, or appears like spam. This often results in:
* The student missing a critical submission deadline.
* A breakdown in the student-teacher relationship.
* A delay in receiving necessary academic support.

### **The Solution**
This could have been easily avoided by using a **clear, descriptive subject line** and a **formal salutation**. 

### **Ambassador Takeaway**
Clear digital communication is not just about politeness; it ensures that the message is received, understood, and acted upon. Mastering this "soft skill" is a vital requirement for any professional engineer working in a global, digital-first environment.
