# Social Media Etiquette for College Students

As a **Student Digital Ambassador**, I have compiled this checklist to help my peers maintain a professional and safe online presence. 

| Category | **Do's (Best Practices)** | **Don'ts (Avoid These)** |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Privacy & Security** | Regularly audit privacy settings and use Two-Factor Authentication (2FA). | Don't post sensitive data like home addresses, phone numbers, or ID cards. |
| **Professionalism** | Use platforms like LinkedIn to showcase your projects, skills, and learning journey. | Don't vent about professors, classmates, or employers on public forums. |
| **Content Quality** | Think before you post. Ask if the content aligns with your future career goals. | Don't engage in online arguments, "trolling," or use offensive language. |
| **Information Integrity** | Verify facts before sharing news to prevent the spread of misinformation. | Don't share unverified links or participate in "spammy" tag-and-share posts. |
| **Digital Citizenship** | Give proper credit when sharing someone else's work or digital assets. | Don't ignore cyberbullying—report harassment if you see it in your feed. |

---

> **Ambassador Note:** Your digital footprint is often a recruiter's first impression of you. Build a brand that reflects your true potential as an engineer.
